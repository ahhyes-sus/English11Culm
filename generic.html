Timeline

Explore the history of land exploitation, the fight for Indigenous independence, and the efforts toward reconciliation and sustainable practices.

Pre-Colonial Era (Before 1500s): Indigenous peoples thrived across North America, living in harmony with the land and maintaining ecological balance.

1500s–1700s: The Colonization Era: European colonization disrupted Indigenous lifestyles through land seizures and fur trading, altering traditional ways of life.

1800s: The Industrial Revolution: Industrialization intensified resource exploitation, forcing Indigenous communities off their lands. The 1876 Indian Act further eroded their land rights and autonomy.

1900s: The Age of Corporate Expansion: Rapid urbanization and resource extraction, such as building pipelines, often damaged Indigenous territories and ecosystems.

1980s–1990s: Rise of Indigenous Advocacy: Court decisions began affirming treaty rights, and the Oka Crisis in 1990 drew attention to unresolved land disputes.

2015: Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC): The Truth and Reconciliation Commission outlined 94 Calls to Action, including a focus on businesses respecting Indigenous lands and collaborating with their communities.

2020s: Reclamation and Corporate Accountability: Movements like Land Back advocate for the return of ancestral territories and sustainable land use, supported by international efforts like the 2022 Global Biodiversity Framework to safeguard ecosystems by 2030.
